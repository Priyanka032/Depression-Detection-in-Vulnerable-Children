About Child Depression

Childhood anxiety and depression often go undiagnosed. If left untreated these conditions, collectively known as internalizing disorders, are associated with long-term negative outcomes including substance abuse and increased risk for suicide.

Anxiety and depression can emerge in children as young as four years old, but symptoms are often overlooked until children can more clearly express their discomfort given the abstract emotions involved, and communicate their impairment with help-seeking adults.
