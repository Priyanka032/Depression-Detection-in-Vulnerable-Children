DIAGNOSIS AND WHAT IT MEANS :

NORMAL

In order to maintain a good mental health of your child:

  • Teach your kids the “language of feelings”.
  • Learn to put yourself in your child’s shoes.
  • Be aware of your child’s overall actions and behaviour.
  • Encourage creativity
  • Show kids that having a mix of struggle and strength is normal for everyone.

ACUTE

Initial stages of depression and your child can be recovered by:

  • Parental help is the key:
    Have a warm, open relationship with your children which help to see what is causing them the trouble. Prioritize on their feelings and ample amount of attention is to be given.
  • Practical help only if the child seeks for it.

MODERATE

  • Parental help is a must.
  • Professional help:
    If your child is having problems at school, a teacher, school nurse, school counsellor or educational psychologist may be able to help.
  • Talking it through:
    Assessments and treatments for children and young people with mental health problems put a lot of emphasis on talking and on understanding the problem in order to work out the best way to tackle it.

HIGH

  • Medication:
    Children need to be assessed by a specialist before they are prescribed any drugs. There is a lot of evidence that talking therapies can be effective for children, but drugs may be also help in some cases.
  • Parental help is neccessary.
  • Professional help also plays a vital role.
